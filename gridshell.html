First 3D printed construction in ice

A team of 9 master students and a professor from Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e) have made a design for world's first 3D printed ice construction ! The gridshell will be built from 15 - 21 December 2018 in Harbin, China.

TU/e students will realise this ice structure, the first 3D printed construction in ice, in the ice capital of the world, Harbin. This Chinese city is well known for its international ice and snow sculpture festival. The gridshellr is located at campus of the Harbin Institute of Technology. In previous years Dutch students realized in Finland various ice structures as well: the Pykrete Dome in 2014, the Sagrada Familia in Ice in 2015 and the Bridge in Ice 2016. Furthermore, they realized a small pilot of the Flamenco Ice Tower on the architectural campus of HIT China last winter.

Currently the team is Harbin to built the gridshell! At this page you can follow the building progress !
